Project Overview

This project analyzes a weather dataset and explores patterns in humidity, temperature, wind speed and pressure. Graphs were created using Python (Pandas, Matplotlib).

Dataset Information

Rows:68474

Columns:22

  • datetime_utc:Date & time recorded in UTC format
  • _conds:Weather conditions(Clear, Rain, Foggy, Cloudy)
  • _dewptm:Dew point
  • _fog:Fog presence(1 = fog, 0 = no fog)
  • _hail:Hail presence(1 = Hail, 0 = no hail)
  • _heatindexm:Heat index value (feels-like temperature)
  • _hum:Humidity percentage (%)
  • _precipm:Precipitation amount
  • _pressurem:Atmospheric pressure
  • _rain:Rain indicator (1 = rain, 0 = no rain)
  • _snow:Rain indicator (1 = snow, 0 = no snow)
  • _tempm:Temperature (main air temperature)
  • _thunder:Thunder presence (1 = thunder, 0 = no)
  • _tornado:Tornado indicator (1 = tornado conditions, 0 = no)
  • _vism:Visibility measurement
  • _wdird:Wind direction in degrees
  • _wdird:Wind direction as text
  • _windchillm:Wind chill temperature (feels colder due to wind)
  • _wspdm:Wind speed (average wind speed)

Data Visualizations

Temprature Over Time

  • Temperature varies moderately Mean ≈ 25.45°C and Std ≈ 8.48 This tells you temperatures usually revolve around 25°C,but can fluctuate by about ±8°C. So the climate seems neither too extreme nor perfectly stable — moderate variation is present.
  • Min = 1°C (rarely this low unless winter/abnormal event) Max = 90°C → This is unrealistic for natural weather conditions This suggests either recording error or faulty sensor readings for certain entries. These outliers should be handled before modeling.
  • zig-zag pattern every year indicates seasonal variation (summer=high temp,winter=low temp)
  • peak shows hotter months, dips shows colder months
  • unusually long spikes represents extreme heat conditions/data anamoly/sensor error/heat waves
  • cycles appear consistent

Humidity Over Time

  • Average humidity is moderate Mean ≈ 51.8% and Median (50%) is close to mean.This shows humidity distribution is fairly balanced, not heavily skewed — typically mid-range humidity.
  • Min = 4% → very dry conditions
  • Max = 99% → extremely humid (almost saturated air)

Pressure Variation

  • restricted the range to 900–1100 hPa, which is the true atmospheric range on Earth under normal conditions, as there were large anamoly values.
  • Min = 929 hPa and Max = 1099 hPa. Mean ≈ 1008, Median (50%) = 1008 and Std = 7.34
  • Shows zig-zag pattern due to continuos shifts between high and low pressure , indicating normal weather variations over time.
  • unusually long spikes represents data anamoly/sensor error

Wind Speed Over Time

  • Mean ≈ 10.84 mph ,Median = 9.3 mph and Max ≈ 192 mph---Possible extreme storm or anamoly
  • Occasional extreme spikes represent- Severe storms /cyclones/Hurricane-like weather events/Sensor or data recording errors
  • no long-term upward or downward trend, meaning wind intensity has not consistently increased or decreased over the years
  • wind speeds may increase during monsoon or storm seasons, but stay calmer in summers/winters.

Conclusion

  • The analysis of the dataset reveals that although a large volume of weather information was available, several columns initially contained missing values, incorrect entries, and unrealistic sensor readings. Through step-by-step data cleaning — especially for temperature, humidity, pressure, and wind speed — the dataset was refined into a reliable and usable form.
  • The dataset, after cleaning, clearly suggests that the recorded region/time period experienced:Moderate temperature,Balanced humidity, Stable atmospheric pressure,Low rainfall,High levels of haze and smoke
